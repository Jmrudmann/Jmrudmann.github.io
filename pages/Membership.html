Membership

West Fork Church Membership

Although other congregations have an official membership roll, at this time in the life of WFF we do not have an official membership or membership process. There are no ‘official’ church membership requirements at WFF as the scriptures do not specifically dictate that as a command. However, we consider those who are continuing with us in serving and attending regularly, by doing so, are outwardly proclaiming they are a part of the local body of Christ at WFF. They are under the privileges and responsibilities as set forth in the scriptures for all New Testament Churches. The Elders of WFF will still speak with you concerning your testimony, your commitment to Jesus Christ, to His Bride (to WFF), and of our commitment as a Church family to you.

We do not have an official ‘Church covenant of commitment’ among the members, but we do anticipate and expect those who enter into the life and ministry of WFF to live a life, to the best of their ability – by the power of the Holy Spirit. A life that is committed to honoring the Lord and living out His truths in such a way, that God is glorified and others around them are impacted by the Gospel of Jesus Christ through their daily lives.

We, at West Fork Fellowship desire to Love One Another By:

The Practice of Christian Love

Matthew 22:39, Matthew 5:43-48, Galatians 6:10, 1 John 3:16-18

The Practice of Christian Ministry

1 Corinthians 12:14-19, Ephesians 4:11-12, Romans 15:14

The Practice of Christian Evangelism

Matthew 28:19-20, Romans 15:20-21, Isaiah 49:6

By God’s grace, will you commit to demonstrate love toward all people through proclaiming the Gospel to the lost, reaching out to minister to our neighbors, and especially by caring for the needs of your fellow brothers and sisters at WFF?

The Practice of Christian Living

(Righteous or holy Living) 1 John 2:3-6, Romans 12:1-2, Matthew 7:17-23

Pursuit of Christian Growth

Ephesians 4:11-16, Philippians 3:12, Hebrews 12:1-2

By God’s grace, will you commit to grow in the grace and knowledge of Jesus Christ that you might live a life that is above reproach and is worthy of the Gospel?

The Pattern of Christian Family Life

Genesis 2:18-25, Ephesians 5:22-33, Ephesians 6:1-3, Romans 1:26-27

By God’s grace, will you commit to esteem the biblical view of marriage and seek to raise your children in the discipline and instruction of the Lord?

Purpose to Resolving Conflicts Biblically

Proverbs 19:11, Matthew 18:21-35, Matthew 18:15-17, Luke 17:3-4

By God’s grace, will you commit to resolve conflicts biblically, refuse to gossip, and support the practice of loving church discipline?

Practice of Christian Giving

2 Corinthians 8:11-12, 1 Corinthians 16:1-2, 2 Corinthians 9:3-12

By God’s grace, will you commit to be a faithful steward of your finances and possessions so that the church may fulfill its responsibility to spread the Gospel to all people?

We, at West Fork Fellowship, commit to the best of our ability to:

Faithfully teach and preach the Word of God

2 Timothy 3:15, 4:1-4; 1 Peter 2:2; Ephesians 5:26

We also seek to serve you by:

Providing opportunities for growth, nurturing, and service

Ephesians 4:12-14; 2 Timothy 1:6; 1 Corinthians 12:4-7

Providing a framework for building fellowship and godly relationships

Ephesians 4:16; 1 Corinthians 12:12-27; Romans 12:3-21

Carrying out our responsibility to reprove, exhort, care for, and discipline

Ephesians 4:14-16; 2 Timothy 3:16-17; 1 Thessalonians 5:14; Galatians 6:1-5; Matthew 18:15-17

Broadening the believer’s concern and perspective toward those unreached by the Gospel in our community and around the world

Matthew 28:18-20; Romans 1:14-17; Philippians 1:27-28
